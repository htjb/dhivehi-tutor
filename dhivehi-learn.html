Vowels in Thaana are written as diacritics above or below a consonant — never as standalone letters. Shown here on އ (Alifu), the neutral vowel carrier. Tap any to hear the sound.

💡 Key Facts about Dhivehi
SCRIPT DIRECTION
Written right-to-left, like Arabic. The script is called Thaana (ތާނަ)
PRONUNCIATION TIP
Vowels are written as diacritics (marks) above or below consonants, similar to Arabic harakat
FORMAL vs INFORMAL
Dhivehi has formal and informal registers. For conversation with a partner, the informal is most natural
LOANWORDS
Many English, Arabic, and Hindi words are used in modern Dhivehi — you'll recognise more than you think!
